Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Knee Osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease that affects the knee. It occurs when the protective cartilage in the knee wears down over time, leading to pain, stiffness, and swelling in the joint.

Common symptoms include knee pain, stiffness, swelling, and limited range of motion. The symptoms can worsen over time, making movement more difficult.

Prevention strategies include maintaining a healthy weight, staying active with low-impact exercises, avoiding repetitive strain, and strengthening the muscles around the knee joint to reduce stress on it.

Management of Knee OA includes physical therapy, medications to reduce pain and inflammation, lifestyle changes like weight management, and in some cases, surgical interventions like knee replacement.
